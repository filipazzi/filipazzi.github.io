About Me

Hello! I am an assistant professor in mathematics at Duke University. I am an algebraic geometer. In particular, my main interests lie in birational geometry.

Before coming to Duke, I was a collaborateur scientifique at the EPFL's Chair of Algebraic Geometry under the direction of Zsolt Patakfalvi and a Hedrick Assistant Adjunct Professor at UCLA under the supervision of Burt Totaro.

I obtained my Ph.D. at the University of Utah under the supervision of Christopher D. Hacon.

Before that, I completed my bachelor's and my master's in Mathematics at the University of Pavia.
